Facts about the breed

- Ridgebacks are also called African Lion Hounds and were originally bred as farm and hunting dogs

-This breed is very protective, but does surprisingly little barking. Instead, a Ridgeback will place his large muscular body between his human and a perceived threat. He doesn't resort to growling, barking or attacking as his first option. Ridgies are standoffish with strangers, so they need to be well socialized at an early age to prevent their naturally protective nature from turning into aggression.

-Adult Ridgebacks measure 24 to 27 inches at the shoulder, weigh 75 to 80 pounds, and their average lifespan is 10 to 12 years. As for breed characteristics, Ridgies rank highest for being affectionate, good with children, and intelligent. They tend to rank lower in friendliness to strangers, and dogs and cats they don't know.

-The name Ridgeback stems from the ridge that run down the spine of this breed, it is usually the first thing that you would notice.
